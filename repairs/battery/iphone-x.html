iPhone X Battery Replacement Tutorial

This battery replacement tutorial slows the job down for first-time repairers: open the display carefully, disconnect power early, track every bracket screw, release adhesive safely, and test the phone before closing it.

iPhone X battery replacement for first-time repairers

What this tutorial does

Use this page when you want to replace a worn battery that has poor runtime, unexpected shutdowns, or swelling risk. Read one step, compare the photo to your phone, complete that action, then move to the next step.

Main risk

A lithium-ion battery can be dangerous if punctured, bent hard, overheated, or pried with a sharp metal tool.

Do before opening

  • Power the iPhone off and clear a bright workspace.
  • Keep a screw mat or labeled paper map beside the phone.
  • Read the screw table before removing brackets with mixed lengths.

Before closing

Before closing the phone, confirm the battery connector is seated, no adhesive liner or loose screw remains inside, and the device boots from the new battery.

New repairer rule: never force a connector, battery adhesive, display edge, or glass fragment just because the next photo looks simple. Stop, re-check the model-specific photo, and keep every screw in its original location.

Before you begin, discharge your iPhone battery below 25%. A charged lithium-ion battery can catch fire and/or explode if accidentally punctured. Power off your iPhone before beginning disassembly. 47

Battery removal aid

Beginner check: Use patience here. Softened adhesive and shallow tools are safer than forcing the panel open.

If any adhesive strips broke off and the battery remains firmly attached to the rear case, prepare a heat pack or use a hair dryer to heat the rear case directly behind the battery. Heat the iPhone until the rear case is slightly too hot to comfortably touch. Don't overheat the iPhone, or you may accidentally ignite the battery. Flip the iPhone back over and thread a strong piece of string (such as dental floss or a length of thin guitar string) underneath the battery. Wrap the ends of the string around a cloth (or wear gloves) to protect your fingers. Pull the string from side to side in a sawing motion all along the length of the battery to separate the adhesive. This can take some time since the adhesive is slow to deform, but with patience it will come free. Do not deform or damage the battery. If you choose to use pry tools to lift the battery out of the iPhone, use extreme caution or you may damage the ribbon cables or the wireless charging coil directly underneath the battery.
