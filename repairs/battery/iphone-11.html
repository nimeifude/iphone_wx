iPhone 11 Battery Replacement Tutorial

This battery replacement tutorial slows the job down for first-time repairers: open the display carefully, disconnect power early, track every bracket screw, release adhesive safely, and test the phone before closing it.

iPhone 11 battery replacement for first-time repairers

What this tutorial does

Use this page when you want to replace a worn battery that has poor runtime, unexpected shutdowns, or swelling risk. Read one step, compare the photo to your phone, complete that action, then move to the next step.

Main risk

A lithium-ion battery can be dangerous if punctured, bent hard, overheated, or pried with a sharp metal tool.

Do before opening

  • Power the iPhone off and clear a bright workspace.
  • Keep a screw mat or labeled paper map beside the phone.
  • Read the screw table before removing brackets with mixed lengths.

Before closing

Before closing the phone, confirm the battery connector is seated, no adhesive liner or loose screw remains inside, and the device boots from the new battery.

New repairer rule: never force a connector, battery adhesive, display edge, or glass fragment just because the next photo looks simple. Stop, re-check the model-specific photo, and keep every screw in its original location.

01

Remove the pentalobe screws

Beginner check: Begin here before any opening work. A lower battery charge reduces the consequence of an accidental battery puncture.

Before you begin, discharge your iPhone battery below 25%. A charged lithium-ion battery can catch fire and/or explode if accidentally punctured. Power off your iPhone before beginning disassembly. Remove the two 6.7 mm-long pentalobe P2 screws at the bottom edge of the iPhone. Opening the iPhone's display will compromise its waterproof seals. Have replacement seals ready before you proceed past this step, or take care to avoid liquid exposure if you reassemble your iPhone without replacing the seals.

47

Battery removal aid

Beginner check: Use patience here. Softened adhesive and shallow tools are safer than forcing the panel open.

If the battery remains firmly attached and an appropriate liquid solvent is not available, prepare a heat pack or use a hair dryer to heat the rear case directly behind the battery. Heat the iPhone until the rear case is slightly too hot to comfortably touch, but no more. Flip the iPhone back over and thread a strong piece of string (such as dental floss) underneath the battery. Wear gloves to protect your fingers, or wrap each end of the string around a tool. Pull the string from side to side in a sawing motion to separate the adhesive. This can take some time since the adhesive is slow to deform, but with patience it will come free. Do not deform or damage the battery. Start from the top or bottom edge of the battery, and pull toward the middle. Don't pull the string through the middle third of the battery, or you may damage the ribbon cables or the wireless charging coil directly underneath. If you choose to use pry tools to lift the battery out of the iPhone, use extreme caution or you may damage the ribbon cables or the wireless charging coil directly underneath the battery.
