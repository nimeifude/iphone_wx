iPhone 11 Screen Replacement Tutorial

This beginner-friendly screen replacement tutorial shows how to open the phone, protect the display cables, transfer or disconnect the required parts, and check the new screen before sealing the device.

iPhone 11 screen replacement for first-time repairers

What this tutorial does

Use this page when you want to replace a cracked, dead, flickering, or touch-faulty display. Read one step, compare the photo to your phone, complete that action, then move to the next step.

Main risk

The display edge adhesive is strong and the front sensor, earpiece, or display flex cables can be damaged by deep tools or rushed connector work.

Do before opening

  • Power the iPhone off and clear a bright workspace.
  • Keep a screw mat or labeled paper map beside the phone.
  • Read the screw table before removing brackets with mixed lengths.

Before closing

Before sealing the display, power on briefly and check touch response, brightness, Face ID or sensor behavior when applicable, and the earpiece area.

New repairer rule: never force a connector, battery adhesive, display edge, or glass fragment just because the next photo looks simple. Stop, re-check the model-specific photo, and keep every screw in its original location.

01

Remove the pentalobe screws

Beginner check: Begin here before any opening work. A lower battery charge reduces the consequence of an accidental battery puncture.

Before you begin, discharge your iPhone battery below 25%. A charged lithium-ion battery can catch fire and/or explode if accidentally punctured. Power off your iPhone before beginning disassembly. Remove the two 6.7 mm-long pentalobe P2 screws at the bottom edge of the iPhone. Opening the iPhone's display will compromise its waterproof seals. Have replacement seals ready before you proceed past this step, or take care to avoid liquid exposure if you reassemble your iPhone without replacing the seals.

Screw and part rule: keep parts in removal order. If this step removes a bracket or screw, its original position matters during reassembly.

02

Mark your opening picks

Beginner check: Use patience here. Softened adhesive and shallow tools are safer than forcing the panel open.

If inserted too far, an opening pick can damage your device. Follow this step to mark your pick and prevent damage. Measure 3 mm from the tip and mark the opening pick with a permanent marker. You can also mark the other corners of the pick with different measurements. Alternatively, tape a coin to a pick 3 mm from the tip.

03

Tape over any cracks

Beginner check: Work from the bracket or connector edge shown in the photo. Do not pry under nearby board parts.

If your iPhone has a cracked screen, keep further breakage contained and prevent bodily harm during your repair by taping over the glass. Lay overlapping strips of clear packing tape over the iPhone's screen until the whole face is covered. Wear safety glasses to protect your eyes from any glass shaken free during the repair. If you can't get the suction cup to stick in the next few steps, fold a strong piece of tape (such as duct tape) into a handle and lift the screen with that instead. If all else fails, you can superglue the suction cup to the screen.

Lift the display slightly

Beginner check: Use patience here. Softened adhesive and shallow tools are safer than forcing the panel open.

Pull up on the suction cup with firm, constant pressure to create a slight gap between the front panel and rear case. Insert an opening pick into the gap under the screen and the plastic bezel, not the screen itself. The watertight adhesive holding the display in place is very strong; creating this initial gap takes a significant amount of force. If you're having a hard time opening a gap, apply more heat, and gently rock the screen up and down to weaken the adhesive until you create enough of a gap to insert your tool.

Open the iPhone

Beginner check: Work from the bracket or connector edge shown in the photo. Do not pry under nearby board parts.

Open the iPhone by swinging the display up from the left side, like the back cover of a book. Don't try to fully separate the display yet, as several fragile ribbon cables still connect it to the iPhone's logic board. Prop the display up against something sturdy. During reassembly, lay the display in position, align the clips along the top edge, and carefully press the top edge into place before snapping the rest of the display down. If it doesn't click easily into place, check the condition of the clips around the perimeter of the display and make sure they aren't bent.
