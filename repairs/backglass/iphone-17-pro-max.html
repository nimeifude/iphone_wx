iPhone 17 Pro Max back glass replacement for first-time repairers

What this tutorial does

Use this page when you want to replace damaged rear glass while protecting cameras, wireless charging parts, cables, and the battery. Read one step, compare the photo to your phone, complete that action, then move to the next step.

Main risk

Rear glass work can involve heat, sharp broken glass, camera surrounds, wireless charging hardware, and model-specific internal parts.

Do before opening

  • Power the iPhone off and clear a bright workspace.
  • Keep a screw mat or labeled paper map beside the phone.
  • Read the screw table before removing brackets with mixed lengths.

Before closing

Before final sealing, inspect camera openings, wireless charging parts, flex cables, glass fragments, and every screw location shown in the guide.

New repairer rule: never force a connector, battery adhesive, display edge, or glass fragment just because the next photo looks simple. Stop, re-check the model-specific photo, and keep every screw in its original location.

Safety precautions

Beginner check: Keep sharp tools away from the battery cell and stop if the battery bends, hisses, smells, or heats unexpectedly.

Let your iPhone's battery drain below 25% before starting this repair - charged lithium-ion battery may catch fire if damaged. Unplug all cables from your phone. Hold the power and either volume button and slide to power off your phone.

Apply strips of packing tape to the cracked glass until it's completely covered - his will help keep the glass contained and allow the suction cup to stick. Heat the bottom edge

Beginner check: Use patience here. Softened adhesive and shallow tools are safer than forcing the panel open.

Adhesive secures the back glass to the frame. Heating the glass softens the adhesive, making it easier to separate. Heat a heat pack and lay it on the bottom edge of the back glass for two minutes. Alternatively, you can use a hair dryer or heat gun to heat the back glass until it's slightly too hot to touch. Improper use of a heat gun can destroy the wireless charging assembly and/or battery - ollow the linked instructions carefully.

Apply the replacement adhesive

Beginner check: Use patience here. Softened adhesive and shallow tools are safer than forcing the panel open.

Grab the tab in the corner of the adhesive sheet and peel the liner to expose a third of the adhesive. The exposed adhesive is very sticky. Don't let it touch anything until you're ready to apply it to the frame. If your adhesive has multiple liners, peel the liner that exposes the side that sticks to the frame.

Photo step 17

Beginner check: Use patience here. Softened adhesive and shallow tools are safer than forcing the panel open.

Carefully align and press the exposed edge of the adhesive strip onto the frame. Once the adhesive is pressed into place, you can't reposition it - ou'll have to remove it and start over with new adhesive. Continue peeling away the liner from the adhesive, gently pressing the adhesive into place. If you've correctly aligned the adhesive, the edges will fall perfectly into place. If your adhesive is slightly misaligned, gently pull the long edges into alignment with the frame. If the adhesive begins to crease or wrinkle, remove it and start over with fresh adhesive. If you don't have another set of adhesive strips handy, it's okay to temporarily put your iPhone back together and use it normally without any adhesive. Just keep in mind that your iPhone's water resistance will be compromised until you replace the adhesive.
